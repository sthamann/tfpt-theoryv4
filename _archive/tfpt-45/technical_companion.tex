\begin{abstract}
The Technical Companion is the long toolbox for the TFPT paper series. It holds conventions,
analytic interfaces, auxiliary proofs, extended comparison maps, empirical ledgers, optional
continuations, record algebra, horizon material, transient channels, and the E8 stage atlas. It is
not a stand-alone theorem paper and should not be required reading for the primitive kernel or the
carrier theorem.
\end{abstract}

\begin{tfptfrontbox}

but new claims should be promoted into the appropriate paper rather than hidden here.

\textbf{Not claimed here.} The companion does not replace Papers 1--6 and does not elevate
comparison maps or optional continuations to theorem status.

\textbf{Falsification or audit surface.} The companion fails if it blurs theorem, standard-theorem
interface, empirical comparison, optional continuation, and programmatic target language.

\section{Dyonic Calibration Lemma and Local $\beta_{\mathrm{BH}}(r)$ Intercept}
\label{app:dyonic-calibration}

This appendix records the analytic dyonic calibration model that supports the local achromatic
intercept $\beta_{\mathrm{BH}}(r)$ used in the EHT-class residual-intercept prediction. It is a
\emph{calibration model}, not a physical horizon-scale solution: the goal is to display how the
fixed TFPT coupling $1/(256\pi^4)=16\cthree^4$ is projected onto a magnetised compact-object
geometry through model-dependent geometric weights.

\begin{tfptwarningbox}
This is presented as a worked dyonic calibration model. It is not advertised as a parameter-free
black-hole solution. The TFPT coupling is fixed; the dyonic charges, inflow geometry, and
emission radius are model inputs.
\end{tfptwarningbox}

\subsection{Analytic dyonic toy ansatz}

Consider a static, spherically symmetric ansatz with a magnetised dyonic interior and the
admissible-branch axion field. Working at leading order in the boundary-normalised expansion, the
axion profile, the radial electric field, and the metric function take the closed forms
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:dyonic-axion}
a(r)=a_\infty+\frac{\cthree\,Q_e Q_m}{8\pi^2\,r^2},
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:dyonic-E}
E(r)=\frac{Q_e}{4\pi r^2}-\frac{\cthree^2\,Q_e Q_m^2}{16\pi^3\,r^4},
\end{equation}
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:dyonic-f}
f(r)=1-\frac{2M}{r}+\frac{Q^2}{r^2}+\frac{D}{r^4},
\end{equation}
where $Q^2=Q_e^2+Q_m^2$ is the standard Reissner--Nordström-like contribution and the quartic
correction $D$ is the leading dyonic backreaction generated by the seam sector.

\subsection{Local $\beta_{\mathrm{BH}}(r)$ intercept}

The corresponding local rotation of the linear-polarization angle in the magnetised inflow region
follows from the determinant-line / Chern--Simons response composed with the local $E\!\cdot\!B$
projection, yielding
\begin{equation}
\label{eq:beta-bh-companion}
\beta_{\mathrm{BH}}(r)
=
\frac{Q_e^{\mathrm{eff}}\,Q_m^{\mathrm{eff}}}{256\pi^4\,r^2}
=
16\,\cthree^4\,\frac{Q_e^{\mathrm{eff}}\,Q_m^{\mathrm{eff}}}{r^2}
=
\frac{\deltatop}{3}\,\frac{Q_e^{\mathrm{eff}}\,Q_m^{\mathrm{eff}}}{r^2},
\end{equation}
where $Q_e^{\mathrm{eff}}(x)$ and $Q_m^{\mathrm{eff}}(x)$ encode the local effective dyonic
weights in the bright magnetically dominated regions of the horizon-scale image, and
$\deltatop=48\cthree^4$ is the topological coefficient of the $\alpha$-kernel correction. The
coupling in front is fixed by the same TFPT branch data that fixes $\alpha$ and
$\beta_{\mathrm{rad}}$; the spatial weights are MHD/GR inputs of the source model.

\subsection{Horizon-shift sign correction}
\label{ssec:horizon-shift-correction}

A known wording slip in earlier draft notes describes the $D>0$ shift of \eqref{eq:dyonic-f} as a
``microscopic increase'' of the outer horizon. This is reversed by direct inspection of the
quartic term: with $D>0$, the function $f(r)$ acquires a positive contribution that grows as
$1/r^4$, so the outer root of $f(r)=0$ migrates inward.

\begin{lemma}[Horizon shift sign]
\label{lem:horizon-shift-sign}
Let $f_0(r)=1-2M/r+Q^2/r^2$ have outer horizon $r_+^{(0)}$. For
$f_D(r)=f_0(r)+D/r^4$ with $D>0$ small enough that the outer real positive root persists, the
shifted outer horizon $r_+(D)$ satisfies
\[
r_+(D) \;<\; r_+^{(0)}.
\]
The sign of the dyonic correction therefore \emph{decreases} the radius of the outer horizon, not
increases it, in this regime.
\end{lemma}

\begin{proof}
Implicit differentiation of $f_D(r_+(D))=0$ gives
\[
\frac{dr_+}{dD}=-\frac{1/r_+^{4}}{f_0'(r_+)+(\partial_r D/r^4)|_{r_+}}.
\]
At the outer horizon $f_0$ crosses zero from below to above with $f_0'(r_+^{(0)})>0$, so the
denominator is positive at $D=0$ and the numerator is positive, giving $dr_+/dD<0$.
\end{proof}

\subsection{Calibration scope}

\begin{remark}[What this lemma is not]
Equation \eqref{eq:beta-bh-companion} is presented as an \emph{analytic dyonic calibration
lemma}. It is not advertised as a realistic black-hole solution and does not replace a full
GRMHD/synchrotron forward model. Its only purpose in the TFPT layer is to display the structural
$1/r^2$ profile and the fixed coupling $1/(256\pi^4)$ that enter the achromatic residual
intercept of the linear polarization angle.
\end{remark}

\section{External RG Fingerprint Protocol}
\label{app:rg-fingerprints}

This appendix records an external stress-test protocol for the closed-branch admissibility data,
formulated as a set of RG fingerprints to be reproduced by an independent two-loop renormalisation
solver (e.g. \texttt{PyR@TE}) seeded with the TFPT input package. The fingerprints are
\emph{external comparison} targets, not internal theorem claims: their purpose is to give a
sceptical reader a clean numerical reproduction surface.

\begin{tfptwarningbox}
The RG fingerprints are presented as an external comparison protocol, not as primitive input.
They become trustworthy only after independent two-loop reproduction with the declared
configuration; until then they are a planned compass with no fixed north.
\end{tfptwarningbox}

\subsection{TFPT branch input package}

The configuration handed to the external solver freezes the closed-branch admissibility data
\[
\cthree=\frac{1}{8\pi},\qquad
\phiz=\frac{1}{6\pi}+\frac{3}{256\pi^4},\qquad
\alpha^{-1}(0)=137.0359992168\ldots,\qquad
b_1=\frac{41}{10},
\]
together with the carrier rank data $(\dim E_-, \dim E_+)=(3,2)$, family multiplicity
$N_{\mathrm{fam}}=3$, admissible occupancy $\Omega_{\mathrm{adm}}=48$, and Higgs index $N_\Phi=1$
from Paper~2. The two-loop SM running is performed inside the standard
$(g_1,g_2,g_3,y_t,\lambda)$ system with the declared threshold map.

\subsection{Four fingerprints}

The fingerprint targets to be reproduced by the external solver are:

\begin{enumerate}[leftmargin=1.5em]
    \item \textbf{Strong-coupling crossing at the seed value.} On the canonical branch the
    two-loop strong coupling crosses $\phiz$ at PeV scales,
    \[
    \alpha_3(\mu_1)\;\approx\;\phiz,
    \qquad
    \mu_1\;\approx\;1\,\mathrm{PeV}.
    \]

    \item \textbf{Strong-coupling crossing at the seam constant.} It crosses $\cthree$ in the
    intermediate corridor,
    \[
    \alpha_3(\mu_2)\;\approx\;\cthree,
    \qquad
    \mu_2\;\approx\;2.5\!\times\!10^{8}\,\mathrm{GeV}.
    \]

    \item \textbf{G8 bridge.} Above
    \[
    M_{G8}\;\approx\;1.8\!\times\!10^{10}\,\mathrm{GeV}
    \]
    the boundary-polarised admissibility data permits a G8 bridge as a downstream scale-grammar
    interface, not as a primitive new particle. The bridge feature is at the level of slope
    grammar, not of phenomenological resonances.

    \item \textbf{Unification corridor.} The three SM gauge couplings approach a unification
    corridor at
    \[
    \mu_{\mathrm{unif}}\;\approx\;1.43\!\times\!10^{15}\,\mathrm{GeV},
    \qquad
    \frac{\Delta\alpha_i}{\alpha_i}\Big|_{\mathrm{spread}}\;\approx\;1.23\%.
    \]
\end{enumerate}

\subsection{Status discipline}

\begin{remark}[Fingerprints, not theorems]
The four items above must be presented as \emph{external comparison fingerprints} of the closed
branch through standard SM running, not as new theorem-level claims. They are reproducible from a
declared \texttt{PyR@TE} configuration that uses only the upstream TFPT inputs listed above.
Until that reproduction is published as a companion artifact, the fingerprints are programmatic
targets.
\end{remark}

\begin{remark}[E8 as downstream scale grammar]
The G8 bridge of fingerprint~3 is recorded at the level of scale grammar inherited from the E8
discussion of the source draft. It is \emph{not} reintroduced as a primitive cause in the
boundary kernel; the boundary-polarised closed branch already determines $\alpha$, the carrier
rank, the family count, and the admissibility complex without invoking E8 as an upstream input.
\end{remark}
