\begin{tcolorbox}[colback=tfptblue!2, colframe=tfptblue!45, title={One-sentence claim}, colbacktitle=tfptblue!65]
TFPT claims that a one-sided boundary datum canonically fixes a primitive kernel, a rigid
$3+2$ carrier packet, an admissible physical sector, and a closed branch $\Tstar$ whose observable
readouts are then compared through explicit sector maps rather than fitted one by one.
\end{tcolorbox}

\section*{Minimal Chain}
\[
\mathfrak S_{\min}\Rightarrow\mathcal B_{\min}\Rightarrow\Tbound
\Rightarrow(\taudbl,\iC,\Pprim,[u_\Sigma],\cthree)
\Rightarrow d^\star_{\mathrm{disc}}
\Rightarrow\Padm
\Rightarrow\mathcal Z_{\mathrm{cl}}=\{x^\star_{\mathrm{cl}}\}
\Rightarrow\Tstar .
\]
The three decoders keep the theory readable:
\[
Y\ \text{generates structure},\qquad [u_\Sigma]=1\ \text{generates counting},\qquad
u=\phiz\ \text{is only a UV shadow after sectorization}.
\]
The carrier theorem fixes
\[
6Y^2-Y-\mathbf1=0,\quad \tr_EY=0
\quad\Rightarrow\quad
E=E_3\oplus E_2,\quad
Y=\operatorname{diag}(-1/3,-1/3,-1/3,1/2,1/2),
\]
and hence
\[
G_{\mathrm{phys}}=\frac{SU(3)\times SU(2)\times U(1)_Y}{\mathbb Z_6},
\qquad
S^+=\Lambda^{\mathrm{even}}E,\qquad
N_{\mathrm{fam}}=3,\quad \Oadm=48,\quad N_\Phi=1.
\]

\section*{What This Is Not}
The prediction papers do not treat every row as the same kind of truth. Some are theorem-level
null tests, some are physical observables, some are scheme projections, and some are downstream
comparison targets. Each standalone prediction paper states its dependency class and kill criterion
before giving the formula chain.
