NICER Target-of-Opportunity request (DRAFT -- prepared in advance, submit on trigger)
======================================================================================

Title:     Daily-cadence timing of the next Vela glitch recovery inside the
           first 3 days: closing the early-window gap of a preregistered
           log-periodic comb search
Target:    PSR B0833-45 / J0835-4510 (Vela pulsar), RA 08h35m20.6s, Dec -45d10m34s
Trigger:   a new Vela glitch reported by the live radio monitoring programmes
           (Jodrell Bank glitch table; IAR/PuMA southern monitoring, which
           detected the 2021 and 2024 glitches within hours), with glitch age
           < 3 days at ToO activation. Vela glitches recur every ~3 years; the
           last giant glitch was 2024 April 29 (MJD 60429.9, dnu/nu = 2.4e-6).

Scientific case
---------------
We run a preregistered, frozen-parameter search for a log-periodic modulation
("comb") in post-glitch spin recoveries: residuals to the standard multi-
exponential recovery model are tested for a component cos(omega * ln tau) at
the fixed angular log-frequency omega = 2pi / ln((3/2)^6) = 2.5827, with a
predicted fractional amplitude epsilon = exp(-pi^2 / ln((3/2)^6)) ~ 0.0173.
Both numbers are frozen in preregistration files before any data contact
(hypotheses/pulsar_pg07_v1.yaml, pulsar_pg08_v1.yaml of the analysis
repository); no parameter is fitted to the data. A null result is a valid,
published outcome of this programme; a detection would establish discrete
scale invariance in neutron-star interior relaxation.

Machine-checked injection studies on real sampling and real noise show the
comb periodogram localises omega only when the recovery spans more than ~2.8
comb periods in ln(tau), i.e. about three decades in post-glitch time tau.
Our complete reduction of the full NICER Vela archive (665 observations,
~762 ks, 2017-2025; 491 usable, 119 piecewise-coherent frequency segments,
median sigma_nu ~ 0.6 microHz; the published 2019 and 2021 glitch steps are
reproduced at 96% and 101% as a sanity gate) quantifies the gap precisely:
the first usable observation sits at tau = 3.4 d (2019 glitch) and 7 d (2021),
so the achieved reach is 2.17 and 1.38 comb periods -- BELOW the 2.8-period
gate. The archive is missing exactly the early window; stacking more late-time
data buys amplitude, not ln(tau) range. Radio data releases hit the same wall
(the public post-2021 Vela ToAs start at tau = 10 d). A trigger inside 3 days
-- ideally with the first exposure at tau ~ 0.5-1 d -- followed by daily
cadence is therefore the ONLY route to a sharp test: first exposure at
tau ~ 0.5 d plus the ongoing long-baseline monitoring out to tau ~ 350-900 d
yields ln(tau) reach of 2.9-3.3 periods, clearing the gate for the first time.

Observation request
-------------------
1. Activation:    within 3 days of a reported Vela glitch (goal: < 1 day).
2. Cadence:       daily visits for the first ~14 days post-glitch (the fast
                  recovery terms have tau_d ~ 0.4-17 d; daily sampling resolves
                  the decades ln(tau) = -0.3 ... 1.2 that no archive covers),
                  then hand-over to the regular monitoring cadence.
3. Exposure:      1-2 ks per visit. In our full-archive reduction, segments
                  built from 1-2 ks of NICER data reach a median frequency
                  uncertainty sigma_nu ~ 0.6 microHz -- sufficient, since the
                  early-recovery transient signal is at the 10-100 microHz
                  level for a giant (dnu/nu ~ 2e-6) Vela glitch.
4. Total new time: ~14-28 ks (14 visits x 1-2 ks) -- comparable to two weeks
                  of the existing Vela monitoring load, but time-critical.
5. Configuration: standard timing mode; soft band 0.2-1.2 keV carries the
                  pulsed signal (band choice validated on the archive).

Analysis plan and references
----------------------------
The reduction and detection pipeline is already built and validated end-to-end
on the full NICER Vela archive (barycentring validated against PINT to 3.4
microseconds RMS; Z^2 segment fits; frozen comb battery with off-kernel
look-elsewhere control; surrogate-calibrated injections). Preregistration:
hypotheses/pulsar_pg07_v1.yaml and pulsar_pg08_v1.yaml (frozen kernel, null
batteries, kill / data-limited criteria). Archive quantification:
results/pg06b_full_vela.json in the analysis repository. External data
context: Basu et al. 2022 (JBO glitch catalogue); Zubieta et al. 2024, A&A
(IAR/PuMA daily monitoring and glitch data release); LVK 2024 Vela glitch
data release, Zenodo doi:10.5281/zenodo.17735648 (phase-connected 2024
ephemeris used to validate our recovery reconstruction).
